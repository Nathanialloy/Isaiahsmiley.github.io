Pertussis, known as whooping cough is a disease caused by a bacterium, it mostly affects children by damaging

the respiratory tract. The whooping cough virus is mostly common in infants, but people of all ages could contract it.

At first, whooping cough causes mild cold-like symptoms that last for 1 to 2 weeks, such as a runny nose, sneezing,

a low-grade fever, fatigue, a mild cough, and watery eyes. After some time people start to develop a chronic cough
